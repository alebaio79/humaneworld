A crisis for animals, the environment and public health.

On factory farms, baby calves are ripped away from their mothers and confined in cages so small they can barely move. Mother pigs spend their entire lives locked up in claustrophobic crates where they cannot even turn around. Such conditions cause incredible stress which often results in physical injury as animals desperately attempt to escape the torment of extreme confinement.

Factory farming causes billions of animals to suffer each year—but they are not the only ones paying a heavy price. Animal agriculture accounts for a large percentage of the greenhouse gases associated with climate change. Additionally, harmful waste from factory farms makes its way into our water supply, poisoning people and our planet.

It does not have to be this way. The HSUS is waging campaigns to persuade corporations to raise their farm animal welfare standards, successfully campaigning to pass farm animal protection legislation, and so much more.
