Rhode Island: End animal testing for cosmetics

Rabbits, mice, rats and guinea pigs continue to suffer and die in experiments to test cosmetics like lipstick, deodorant and shampoo. Cosmetics that are both safe and humane can be manufactured using thousands of existing ingredients—and new ingredients can be tested using non-animal tests. These non-animal alternatives are often cheaper, faster and more relevant to humans, and therefore more reliable predictors of safety.

More than 30 countries and eight U.S. states have already passed laws preventing the sale of cosmetics that have been newly tested on animals. Companies that wish to sell cosmetics in those countries and states have already adopted non-animal test methods for their products and ingredients.
