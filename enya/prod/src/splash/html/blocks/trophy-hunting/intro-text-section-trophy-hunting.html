You can help protect our world’s most majestic animals.

American trophy hunters pay big money to kill animals overseas and import more than 126,000 wildlife “trophies” per year on average. They also do their blood sport–killing domestically: More than 100,000 native carnivores (bears, bobcats, mountain lions and wolves) are killed every year by trophy hunters. Barbaric wildlife killing contests still take place regularly in 42 U.S. states, slaughtering hundreds of bobcats, coyotes, foxes, and even mountain lions at a single event. These mass killings damage entire ecosystems and can push species to the brink of extinction.

Who allows this to happen—and why? There are many myths and misconceptions that trophy hunts can help with “population control” or “conservation” efforts. We expose the truth and work with governments to ban wildlife killing contests, trophy hunting, and imports of animal body parts. We find and implement humane solutions to human-wildlife conflicts.

The HSUS is the country’s most effective animal protection organization. Together with our supporters, we protect wildlife and their natural ecosystems. Please donate now to help.
